About Me

I am a dedicated Data Scientist and Artificial Intelligence researcher with a robust background in applying cutting-edge machine learning techniques to healthcare. My work primarily focuses on leveraging federated learning and explainable AI to enhance patient care and healthcare systems. With extensive experience in both research and practical applications, I am committed to advancing technology that can provide tangible benefits in medical settings.

My career has been characterized by a blend of rigorous academic research and real-world problem-solving. I hold a Ph.D. in Computer Science, during which I concentrated on the intersection of AI and healthcare. I am passionate about developing technologies that not only push the boundaries of what AI can achieve but also address critical issues such as data privacy and interpretability.

Work Experience

At IDM, I work on the SAFICU project, which aims to revolutionize patient care in Intensive Care Units (ICUs) by applying federated learning techniques. This project involves developing AI models that can work across multiple institutions while preserving patient privacy. My role includes conducting advanced research on federated learning and explainable AI, mentoring students, and contributing to high-impact publications and international conferences.

Key responsibilities include:

  • Designing and implementing federated learning frameworks for real-time ICU monitoring.

  • Overseeing the research progress of M.Sc. and B.Sc. students, guiding them through their projects.
  • Publishing findings in leading journals and presenting at global conferences to share insights and advancements.
