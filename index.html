Projects

SAFICU Project

The SAFICU project is an innovative research initiative aimed at applying federated learning to improve patient care in ICUs. This project involves developing a collaborative learning system that enables multiple hospitals to train models on their data without sharing it directly. The goal is to enhance predictive accuracy and support better clinical decisions while ensuring data privacy.

Achievements of the SAFICU project:

  • Implemented a federated learning framework that respects data privacy regulations.
  • Improved patient monitoring and outcome prediction through collaborative model training.
  • Facilitated cross-institutional research collaborations, leading to richer and more diverse datasets.

Clinical Care Datathon

In 2019, I participated in the Clinical Care Datathon in Milan, where I led a team to develop an AI solution that addressed critical issues in clinical care. Our project won first prize, sponsored by Google, for its innovative approach to using AI to optimize patient care processes and outcomes.

Project highlights include:

  • Developed an AI-based solution that leveraged machine learning to streamline clinical workflows.
  • Presented our findings and technology at the datathon, receiving recognition for its impact on clinical care.
  • Applied advanced AI techniques to real-world problems, demonstrating practical applications of our research.
